Art & Design A Level

Vibrant and exciting department where students develop life-long love of visual expression. Choose between Fine Art and 3D Design courses suited to students who enjoy individual voice and creative thought.

"The visual arts is an area in which our students develop their personal expression in this fast-moving world"

— Jo Millar, Head of Art

Fine Art & 3D Design Pathways

Fine Art: Develop skills through increased intensity of study. Learn traditional and contemporary practice and media, developing individual skills to suit your creative vision. Explore painting, drawing, ceramics, glass fusing, installation, sound-scaping, photography, printmaking, resin and plaster work.

3D Design: Foundation-style course covering diverse techniques and processes. Develop appreciation of formal elements: solid, void, form, shape, texture, colour, decoration, surface treatment, scale, proportion, structure, rhythm and movement.

Cultural Context: Study relevant images, artefacts and resources from past and present, including diverse cultural perspectives. Historical and contextual studies embedded throughout.

Assessment & Facilities

Component 1 (60%): Coursework portfolio and independent essay
Component 2 (40%): Externally set assignment

Specialist Equipment: Fine art materials, clays, easels, photography studio, iPads and Apple pens, projectors, printing presses, kilns, potter's wheel, dedicated Art Library

  • Cultural and historical awareness

Drama & Theatre Studies A Level

Inspires students to become independent theatre-makers with skills for higher education and beyond. Emphasising practical creativity alongside research and theoretical understanding through experience.

"Theatre is the most immediate and most dynamic of all art forms, giving a voice to the voiceless"

— Sian McBride, Head of Theatre Studies

Comprehensive Theatre Training

AQA Drama and Theatre Course: Three-component structure including open book examination, original drama creation, and practical performance work. Content taught through group work, discussion and practical workshops.

Text Analysis & Performance: Study two texts with analysis and evaluation of live theatre performances. Practically explore texts including opportunities for design, direction, and performance perspectives.

Devised Theatre: Participate in group creation of original theatre influenced by major practitioners. Develop collaborative and creative skills essential for theatre-making.

Practical Performance Assessment

Making Theatre: Practical exploration of three contrasting scripted extracts, culminating in externally examined performance

Specialist Facilities: Suite of specialist rooms, black box theatre, dance studio, dedicated classrooms

Professional Exposure: Workshops from visiting practitioners, numerous live performances locally and in West End

Performance Skills

  • Acting technique and character development
  • Directing and creative leadership
  • Technical theatre and stage management
  • Devising and collaborative creation

Music A Level

Relevant and contemporary qualification offering chance to study wide range of musical genres. From Hans Zimmer to Renaissance music - continually refreshed and current, taught in 'All-Steinway School' facilities.

"Music is constantly evolving, inspiring creativity and expression in a way that no other subject can"

— Shaun Pirttijarvi, Head of Music

Contemporary Music Education

Modern Repertoire: Study contemporary composers like Hans Zimmer (No Time To Die, Dune soundtracks) alongside classical masters. Course embraces all genres from film music to traditional compositions.

Integrated Approach: Three components covering aural perception, appraising, analysis, performance and composition work together, where analysis feeds into performance and composition.

Personalised Assessment: 60% NEA (Non-Exam Assessment) allows exceptional personalisation, accommodating diverse musical interests and strengths.

Three-Component Structure

Component 1 - Appraising Music: External assessment via 2.5-hour paper of listening and analysis
Component 2 - Performance: External assessment of recordings made March-May of Upper Sixth year
Component 3 - Composition: Two pieces composed and externally assessed

Entry Requirement: Music notation reading at minimum ABRSM Grade 5

Musical Excellence

  • Composition and arranging skills
  • Music analysis and critical listening
  • Cultural and historical understanding

History of Art A Level

Study of Art in historical and contemporary forms provides crucial knowledge of world civilisations. Learn visual and analytical skills applicable in many walks of life, understanding how images shape social and political identities.

"History of Art is an inter-disciplinary subject which transcends Art, Philosophy, History, Science, Religion, Sociology and Anthropology. It is the most enriching and rewarding subject, which is for life"

— Nick Nelson, Head of History of Art

Comprehensive Art Historical Study

Visual Analysis & Themes: Two externally examined papers focusing on visual analysis, identities, and war in art and architecture. Develop skills in interpreting how art reflects and shapes society.

Renaissance to Baroque: Study two major historical periods - "Invention and Illusion: The Renaissance in Italy" and "Power and Persuasion: The Baroque in Catholic Europe." Understanding artistic evolution and cultural context.

Critical Thinking: Demonstrate knowledge of art contexts, analyse and interpret artists' work, understand visual language, and make critical judgements through substantiated argument.

First-Hand Cultural Experience

Gallery Visits: Multiple trips to London and Oxford galleries in Autumn and Summer Terms
International Study Tours: Easter holiday cultural trips to destinations like Rome, Florence, and Venice
Qualification: Pearson Edexcel History of Art A Level (9HT0)

Inter-Disciplinary Excellence

Academic Foundation: Perfect preparation for university study in Art History, Philosophy, Archaeology, History, or Cultural Studies. Develops analytical writing and critical thinking essential for humanities subjects.

Professional Applications: Museum and gallery curation, arts administration, cultural journalism, auction houses, art criticism, and arts education. Skills transfer to law, diplomacy, and cultural institutions.
