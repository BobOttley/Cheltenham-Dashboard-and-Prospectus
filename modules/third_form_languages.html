German at College

Beginners' Course (3 periods/week): German is offered as a beginners' language option for all Third Form pupils. No previous experience is required - you'll start from scratch with comprehensive support and structured progression.

Structured Approach: The course has a topic-based approach combined with clear grammatical progression, ensuring you understand both how German works and how to use it in real contexts.

Authentic Language: German focuses on authentic language put into a cultural context, helping you understand not just vocabulary and grammar but also how German is actually used in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and other German-speaking regions.

Teaching Methods

The course encourages grammatical awareness and more traditional methods of language learning such as regular vocabulary learning as well as more interactive and creative approaches.

This balanced methodology develops both the systematic understanding of German's logical structure and the practical skills needed for authentic communication.

Latin at College

For All Abilities: All pupils in the Third Form study Classics in some form, usually depending on what you have studied before joining College. The Third Form Latin course offers the opportunity for all pupils to access GCSE study, regardless of how much Latin you have done before coming to College.

Academic Benefits: Latin provides academic challenge and the study of the Romans provides intrinsically interesting literature and background. The Latin language itself develops logical thinking, vocabulary skills, and grammatical understanding applicable to all languages.

Strong Tradition: The strength of Cheltenham College's Classical tradition is among the primary features of the school, with specialist teachers and excellent facilities supporting your studies.

EAL at College

If English is not your native language and you are deemed to need support, you will have English as an Additional Language lessons when your peers are learning another language, normally French. In the Third Form, you have three lessons per week together as a class.

EAL lessons aim to equip you with the necessary skills and confidence in the use of English language to allow you to integrate successfully in the mainstream curriculum and achieve your full potential at College.

Teaching Approach

Lower College EAL pupils enjoy the interactive and engaging approach adopted in lessons, aiming to develop your language to a high enough academic standard to cope with the demanding British curriculum, while supporting and consolidating your skills.

The detail of lessons depends on any group's particular needs, as identified through diagnostic testing. However, the focus of the lessons will be on enhancing all four skills: listening, reading, writing and speaking.

Support & Assessment

Cross-Curricular Focus: The department aims to support not only EAL pupils' learning of the language, but also what you are covering in other subjects. To this end there is a strong cross-curricular approach and a focus on developing effective study skills.

Individual Lessons: Individual one-to-one lessons, for which there is an extra charge, are also available if there is need for further support. Those identified with particular needs are encouraged to take extra lessons and your parents/guardians will be informed.

Regular Review: Work is regularly reviewed and together the teacher and yourself will make suggestions and set targets for the term ahead, ensuring continuous progress toward linguistic and academic goals.

Competitions & Opportunities: We are keen to showcase the talents of our pupils and enter you for competitions where possible, including creative writing, poetry, and public speaking competitions.
