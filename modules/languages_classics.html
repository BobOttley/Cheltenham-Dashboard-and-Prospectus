French A Level

Develop linguistic competence while exploring the changing state of French family life, digital world impact, French music and cinema, cultural heritage, immigration, racism, integration, and political engagement. Essential preparation for global careers and cultural understanding.

"At College, we want our students' language learning to open up a world of opportunity."

— Keri Price, Head of Modern Languages

Cultural & Contemporary Studies

Lower Sixth Cultural Exploration: Changing state of family life in France, digital world impact on society, French music and cinema appreciation, and cultural heritage preservation. Topics provide deep insight into contemporary French society and values.

Upper Sixth Social Analysis: Immigration impact, racism and integration challenges, political life aspects including youth engagement with politics and protest movements. Critical examination of current social and political issues affecting French-speaking communities.

Individual Research Project (IRP): Student-chosen presentation on almost any aspect of life or culture in French-speaking countries. Past projects include 'Impact of Fashion by Coco Chanel and Dior' and 'Catalan Independence' - demonstrating the breadth of academic exploration possible.

Assessment Structure

Qualification: AQA French A Level (7652)
Paper 1: Listening, Reading and Writing on cultural and social topics
Paper 2: Essays on set text and film study in target language
Paper 3: Speaking assessment with topic discussion and IRP presentation

Entry Requirements

  • Grade 7 required in French at GCSE
  • Strong linguistic competence demonstrated
  • Cultural curiosity and interest in francophone world
  • Commitment to developing advanced communication skills

Skills Development

  • Advanced linguistic competence in French
  • Cultural and political awareness
  • Independent research and presentation abilities
  • Critical analysis of contemporary social issues

Recommended Cultural Resources

Literature: L'étranger by A. Camus
Un Sac de Billes by Joseph Joffo
Contemporary Media: French news sources, cultural documentaries, music exploration

Latin A Level

Rich blend of language and literature from the ancient world, developing linguistic skills to appreciate some of the world's finest literature. Understanding Roman events, culture, and attitudes while seeing how the ancient world shaped Western civilization.

"Latin offers students a supremely logical and coherent structure and the rich cultural and literary heritage of the Romans."

— Tom Lambert, Head of Classics

Language & Literature Balance

Language Component: Study of grammar, vocabulary, and sentence structure principally tested through translation. Composition into Latin developed as optional but recommended skill. Vocabulary list of approximately 820 words requiring thorough mastery.

Literature Analysis: Study of excerpts from ancient texts (around 440 lines per year), developing skills to answer comprehension, translation, and stylistic questions. Responses range from short answers to extended essay-style work with introduction and conclusion.

Cultural Understanding: Gaining insights into Roman events, culture, and attitudes at significant historical moments. Understanding relationship between classical and modern worlds and how ancient Rome shaped Western civilization.

Rigorous Academic Assessment

Qualification: OCR Latin A Level (H443)
Structure: 50% Language (translation and composition) + 50% Literature (textual analysis)
Vocabulary Requirement: Mastery of approximately 820 essential Latin words
Skills Development: Translation, literary analysis, historical contextualization

Classical Resources

Dynasty by Tom Holland
Latin Love Lessons by Charlotte Higgins
The Metamorphoses by Ovid
Visual Media: Ancient Rome: The Rise and Fall of an Empire (TV series)

Classical Civilisation A Level

Study of the Ancient World entirely in English, offering broad understanding of classical civilizations. Explore how the ancient world reflects modern issues including gender, belief, sexuality, and citizenship while developing transferable analytical and written argument skills.

"Classical Civilisation offers a broad insight into the lives, monuments and myths of the ancient Mediterranean world."

— Tom Lambert, Head of Classics

Three Core Components

The World of the Hero: Study epic poetry of Homer and Virgil across both Lower and Upper Sixth. Explore narratives and characters of the Odyssey and Iliad alongside insights into ancient values and lives, from attitudes to women to reflections on imperial propaganda.

Culture and the Arts: Foundation study of theatre as the basis of modern Western drama. Reading great works of Greek tragedy such as Sophocles' Oedipus and political comedy like Aristophanes' Frogs, using material evidence from pottery and architecture to understand drama's civic role.

Beliefs and Ideas: In-depth study of Greek Religion beyond familiar Olympian gods. Investigation of mysterious cults, majestic sanctuaries, and philosophical thinking of those who challenged orthodox religious belief in the ancient world.

Accessible Assessment Structure

Qualification: OCR Classical Civilisation A Level (H408)
Entry Requirement: Grade 6+ in essay-based subject (English Literature, History, TPE) if no Classics GCSE
No Prior Study Required: Some strongest students begin at A Level without previous Classics experience
Assessment: Three papers combining source analysis with extended thematic essay writing

Classical Department Excellence

Classics Society: Lively student-led society with games, quizzes, and academic talks at twice-termly evening events. Academic Lectures: Rotating programme of annual lectures, competitions, and educational trips. Historic Setting: Six specialist teachers in six classrooms situated in historic central Quad location.

IELTS Preparation Excellence

Four Essential Modules: Comprehensive preparation across Reading, Writing, Speaking, and Listening components. Examination targets academic skills required for courses in higher education through diverse academic subject areas including Environment, Education, and Technology.
