Qualification Options

A Levels: The main school-leaving qualifications in England. These are 'linear' qualifications with public examinations taking place in the Summer Term of Upper Sixth. College organises robust, high-stakes internal examinations during Lower Sixth and Mock A Level exams after Christmas in Upper Sixth to ensure complete preparation.

BTEC & Cambridge Technical Alternatives: Vocational qualifications that directly prepare students for the professional world. Available in Business, Sport, Information Technology, and Applied Psychology. These can be studied as single subjects alongside two or three A Levels, offering greater flexibility and accessibility.

Study Pathways

Three A Levels + EPQ

Study three A Level subjects plus the Extended Project Qualification - providing excellent preparation for university while allowing deep specialisation in chosen fields.

Four A Levels

For academically ambitious students, four A Levels provide maximum flexibility for university applications and demonstrate exceptional academic capability.

Subject Selection Guidance

Core Principle: Students should choose subjects they enjoy most and in which they have the best chance of achieving high grades. Our primary concern is achieving three good grades for first-choice university admission.

GCSE Requirements: Generally Grade 6 or B at GCSE in the subject itself or closely related area. Higher entry requirements apply to certain subjects. Students uncertain about meeting requirements should discuss with relevant Head of Department.

Extended Project Qualification (EPQ)

A cornerstone of Cheltenham College's Sixth Form curriculum, the EPQ develops sophisticated research and project management skills while providing universities with evidence of independent academic capability.

The EPQ will develop your independent research skills essential for university success.

EPQ Project Opportunity for [Child's Name]

Your EPQ project will develop essential research skills while exploring your personal interests in depth.

Project Structure & Benefits

Independent Research: Students step away from A Level syllabus confines to engage in extended, in-depth study of personally chosen topics. Working within small supervision groups led by designated mentors, students research and produce dissertations or practical artefacts.

University Preparation: The EPQ provides excellent preparation for undergraduate study, making projects extremely attractive to universities. Students develop knowledge beyond the curriculum while managing their own learning - exactly what universities expect.

Application Enhancement: Project information supports university applications by individualising personal statements, showcasing depth of subject interest, knowledge, and research skills. Provides valuable discussion points for interviews.

Assessment & Recognition

External Assessment: EPQ is externally assessed and equivalent to half an A Level. Submission occurs at the end of Spring Term in Lower Sixth.

University Value: Some universities consider EPQ in making offers, potentially reducing required entry grades. Demonstrates independent learning capability highly valued in higher education.

Programme Principles

Four core principles underpin the programme: resilience, openness to opportunities, ambition, and health and wellbeing. The programme incorporates preparation for higher education and careers, social, moral, spiritual and cultural development, plus PSHE including relationships and sex education.

Curriculum Content

Lower Sixth Focus

Adjusting to Upper College expectations, examination preparation, cultural awareness, healthy relationships, sexual harassment awareness, consent, and personal safety. Concludes with pre-university week focusing on CVs and personal statements.

Upper Sixth Development

Practical sessions on budgeting, job applications, professional self-marketing, and transition to independence. Designed to explore thoughts and ideas while developing skills in structured, supportive environment.
