Business A Level

Dynamic and engaging subject exploring large and small organisations globally. Perfect foundation for business-related undergraduate study and Russell Group applications.

"The business world is one which is forever shifting and evolving. Studying Business allows you to follow these developments and develop skills and insight that will allow you to become a part of it."

— Holly Brooke, Head of Business

Comprehensive Business Understanding

Business Formation & Growth: Learn how businesses form from startup to global enterprise. Develop entrepreneurial mindset applicable to future leadership roles.

Functional Areas: Explore marketing, HR, finance, and operations interactions. Understanding how vital areas interlink for business success.

Strategic Analysis: Understand internal strengths/weaknesses and external opportunities/threats. Essential analytical skills for leadership development.

Assessment Structure

Qualification: AQA Business A Level (7132)
Assessment: 100% examination - three 2-hour papers
Question Types: Multiple choice, short answers, data responses, extended essays
Entry Requirement: Grade 6 in Mathematics required

Business Skills Development

  • Strategic thinking and analysis
  • Financial literacy and management
  • Marketing and consumer psychology
  • Leadership and team management

Career Applications

  • Entrepreneurship and business ownership
  • Management consulting
  • Financial services and banking
  • Marketing and business development

Computer Science A Level

Develop analytical thinking and problem-solving skills. Computational thinking at its core, providing solutions through modelling, analysing, designing and implementing innovative ideas through coding.

"Computer Science is all about making things as simple as possible, but not simpler"

— Jonathon Morton, Head of Computer Science

Computational Thinking & Problem Solving

Core Principles: Learn computation principles, algorithms, programming, data representation, computer systems, organisation, architecture, communications, networking, and databases.

Creative Computing: Creative subject involving innovative thinking and development through coding. Problem solving requires precision, creativity and careful reasoning.

Ethical Considerations: Appreciate ethical issues with current and emerging computing technologies and their consequences in modern society.

OCR Course Structure

Qualification: OCR Computer Science A Level (H446)
Structure: Two examined units plus Programming Project coursework
Units: Computer Systems, Algorithms and Programming, Programming Project
Entry Requirement: Grade 7 in Mathematics required

Technical Skills

  • Programming and software development
  • Algorithm design and analysis
  • Database design and management
  • Network architecture understanding

University & Career Pathways

  • Computer Science and Software Engineering
  • Engineering and Physics degrees
  • Healthcare technology and business computing
  • AI and cybersecurity

Psychology A Level

Scientific study of human behaviour and experience through attachment, memory, biological psychology, research methods, and individual differences.

"Psychology will not teach you to be a mind-reader, but it will help you understand your fellow humans a little better!"

— Tricia Norman, Head of Psychology

Scientific Study of Human Behaviour

Core Topics: Attachment formation, memory models, eye witness testimony, biological psychology, brain structure, nervous system functions, localization, and biological rhythms.

Research Methods: Practical psychologist training including research design, statistical analysis, and result interpretation with different psychological approaches.

Individual Differences: Abnormality definitions, phobias, depression, OCD characteristics and treatments. Psychological principles applied to everyday life and clinical practice.

Scientific Assessment Structure

Qualification: AQA Psychology A Level (7182)
Entry Requirements: Grade 6 in Science (Biology preferred) and Mathematics
Assessment: Three 2-hour examination papers
Skills Development: Statistical analysis, research methodology, critical evaluation

Research & Analytical Skills

  • Statistical analysis and data interpretation
  • Research methodology and experimental design
  • Critical evaluation of psychological studies
  • Scientific report writing

Professional Applications

  • Clinical and therapeutic psychology
  • Educational and developmental psychology
  • Marketing and consumer behaviour
  • Human resources and management

Sports Science A Level

Deeper understanding of the human body applying scientific theories to optimise health and sporting performance. Multi-disciplinary approach covering physiological, psychological, and sociological factors.

"Sports Science is a fusion of scientific application and analysis combined with a focus on best sporting practice"

— Rebecca Faulkner, Head of Sport Science

Multi-Disciplinary Sports Analysis

Scientific Application: Physiological, psychological and sociological factors influencing athlete performance.

Practical Integration: Theory combined with practical performance provides excellent basis for sports science as academic discipline.

Modular Perspectives: Physiological (Sports Technology, Energy Systems, Newton's Laws), Psychological (Arousal, Aggression, Attribution), Sociological (Elite Performance, Sport and Ethnicity).

Comprehensive Assessment

Qualification: AQA Physical Education A Level (7582)
Theory Component: 70% examination-based
Non-Examined Assessment: 30% - practical (15%) + coursework (15%)
Entry Requirement: Grade 6 in PE or Biology

Scientific Analysis Skills

  • Physiological testing and measurement
  • Sports psychology application
  • Biomechanical analysis techniques
  • Performance optimization strategies

Career Applications

  • Sports physiotherapy and rehabilitation
  • Sports psychology and mental performance
  • Elite athlete performance analysis
  • Sports technology and innovation
