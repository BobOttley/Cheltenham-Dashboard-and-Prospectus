What does the course cover?

The syllabus followed aims towards the IGCSE in Mathematics from Edexcel - Pearson Edexcel International GCSE in Mathematics (Specification A) (4MA1). This develops a broad approach to Mathematics which is relevant and appropriate to the many different uses of the subject in the modern world while maintaining the traditional academic rigour.

In the Third Form, you will recap much of the basic material met previously and then go on to study new material; Algebra and Shape feature strongly in the first term. Whole year group tests occur as needed with usually two in the first term.

Advanced Track

  • Top set aims to sit IGCSE at end of Fourth Form
  • Target Grade 9 (the top grade) in the exam
  • Progress to Additional Mathematics in Fifth Form

Standard Track

  • All other sets sit exam at end of Fifth Form
  • Whole year group often reset Fourth Form entry
  • Solid foundation building approach

Foundation Concepts

Biology Tailored for [Child's Name]

Your biology studies will focus on areas that align with your interests and career aspirations.

Cell Biology: Understanding cell structure and function, both prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, organelles, and cellular processes including respiration and photosynthesis at an introductory level.

Life Processes: Exploring how organisms obtain nutrients, remove waste, respond to stimuli, and reproduce. Foundation concepts in physiology across different organism types.

Genetics & Evolution: Introduction to heredity, DNA structure, genetic variation, and evolutionary processes. Understanding how species change over time and adapt to environments.

IGCSE Preparation

Fundamental Chemistry

Chemistry Learning Path for [Child's Name]

Your chemistry studies will emphasize the connections between molecular understanding and real-world applications.

Atomic Structure: Understanding atoms, elements, and the periodic table. Learning how electron arrangement determines chemical properties and behavior of different elements.

Chemical Bonding: Exploring how atoms combine to form compounds through ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding. Understanding molecular structure and properties.

Chemical Reactions: Introduction to different types of reactions, including acids and bases, oxidation and reduction, and energy changes in reactions.

Core Physics Concepts

Physics & Mathematics for [Child's Name]

Physics will strengthen your mathematical skills while revealing the elegant equations that govern natural phenomena.

Forces and Motion: Understanding how objects move and the forces that cause motion. Introduction to concepts of speed, velocity, acceleration, and Newton's laws of motion.
