Mathematics A Level

Foundation of scientific thinking and language of modern technology. Develops logical thinking, problem-solving skills, and independent mathematical reasoning essential for success in our technological society.

"Mathematics plays a central role in the modern world; it is not only the language of Science and Technology but also a fascinating and challenging subject in its own right."

— Dr Brendan Enright, Head of Mathematics

Course Structure & Content

Pure Mathematics: Algebraic foundation covering Calculus, Co-ordinate Geometry, Vectors, and Numerical Methods. Develops abstract thinking and analytical skills fundamental to mathematical reasoning.

Probability & Statistics: Statistical skills to analyse data and relate findings to probability theory. Study contexts from lottery odds to quality control analysis, developing critical thinking about data interpretation.

Mechanics: Mathematical modelling by simplifying complex problems into solvable formats. Problems involve motion of objects under applied forces, from car movement to skiing dynamics.

Assessment & Examinations

Qualification: Pearson Edexcel Mathematics A Level (9MA0)
Structure: Linear qualification with examinations at end of Upper Sixth
Preparation: Robust internal examinations during Lower Sixth plus Mock A Level exams after Christmas in Upper Sixth

Entry Requirements

  • Grade 8 in Mathematics at IGCSE
  • Strong logical thinking abilities
  • Mental tenacity and abstract reasoning
  • Demonstrated mathematical aptitude

Popular Subject Combinations

  • Physics, Chemistry, Biology (Sciences)
  • Geography, Economics (Social Sciences)
  • History, Modern Languages, English
  • Classics (Classical civilisations)

University Pathways & Career Destinations

Essential for: Engineering (requires Mathematics), Economics, Computer Science, Architecture, and most scientific subjects at top universities.

Highly Advantageous for: Medicine and Dentistry applications to Oxford and Cambridge, Business Studies, Geography, Psychology, and Philosophy.

Career Paths: Engineering, Finance and Banking, Data Science, Research, Technology, Architecture, Actuarial Science, and Academic Research.

Further Mathematics A Level

For students of exceptional mathematical ability who relish academic challenges. Second A Level taken alongside Mathematics, extending studies to greater depth while introducing advanced areas for elite university preparation.

"Further Mathematics opens doors to the most competitive universities and provides unparalleled preparation for mathematics, engineering, and physics degrees at the highest level."

— Mathematics Department, Cheltenham College

Advanced Mathematical Concepts

Core Advanced Topics: Complex Numbers, Matrices, Further Vectors, Proof by Induction, and significantly more challenging mathematical concepts extending far beyond single Mathematics A Level.

Modular Structure: Two compulsory core modules plus two optional specialist papers. Popular choices include Further Pure Mathematics and Further Mechanics, allowing specialisation relevant to university aspirations.

Teaching Approach: Four additional periods per week beyond Mathematics A Level, with intensive small-group teaching maintaining high academic standards.

Assessment & Standards

Qualification: Pearson Edexcel Further Mathematics A Level (9FM0)
Academic Standard: Much more demanding than single Mathematics A Level
Expectation: Only suitable for students virtually certain to achieve A* in Mathematics A Level
University Recognition: Counts as one choice but delivers two A Levels

Entry Requirements

  • Grade 8 in Mathematics at IGCSE (minimum)
  • Almost certain A* prediction in Mathematics A Level
  • Exceptional mathematical aptitude
  • Strong commitment to mathematical challenge

University Advantages

  • Essential for Cambridge Mathematics
  • Highly advantageous for Oxford Mathematics
  • Preferred for top-tier Engineering courses
  • Advantageous for Economics at elite universities

Elite University Preparation

Oxbridge Mathematics: Further Mathematics increasingly essential for competitive Cambridge applications and highly advantageous for Oxford. Advanced concepts provide crucial preparation for undergraduate study.

Engineering Excellence: Top engineering courses at Imperial College, Cambridge, and Oxford highly value or require Further Mathematics, recognising advanced problem-solving skills it develops.

Economics Advantage: Provides significant advantage for mathematical economics and econometrics courses at elite universities.

Physics A Level

Understanding how physical world works through experimental evidence and mathematical analysis. Develops problem-solving abilities and analytical thinking while opening doors to cutting-edge careers in engineering, research, and technology.

"Physics is about using experimental evidence to make sense of the physical world around us."

— Robin Eddie, Head of Physics

Course Content & Structure

Lower Sixth Foundation: Comprehensive understanding of waves, electricity, mechanics, and materials. Introduction to particle physics sets stage for advanced Upper Sixth study.

Upper Sixth Advanced Topics: Circular motion, simple harmonic motion, gravitational fields, magnetic fields, electric fields, and nuclear physics. Optional specialisation units allow exploration of interests like Astrophysics or Engineering Physics.

Practical Physics: Extensive hands-on experimentation developing practical skills, data analysis abilities, and scientific method understanding. Laboratory work integrated throughout with separate practical competency assessment.

Assessment Structure

Qualification: AQA Physics A Level (7408)
Examination Structure: Three papers, each lasting two hours
Practical Assessment: 15% of A Level questions test practical skills
Required Competency: Teacher endorsement of practical skills throughout course

Entry Requirements

  • Grade 7 in Physics or Grade 7/7 in Double Award Science
  • Strong mathematical ability (Grade 7+ in Mathematics recommended)
  • Interest in understanding natural phenomena
  • Analytical and problem-solving aptitude

Beyond the Curriculum

  • Science Society evening events
  • Annual online Physics Brawl competition
  • British Physics Olympiad participation
  • Cheltenham College Astronomical Society

Career Pathways & University Study

Engineering Requirements: Physics essential or highly recommended for most engineering disciplines, particularly when combined with Mathematics. Provides fundamental understanding of physical principles underlying all engineering applications.

Scientific Careers: Direct pathway to university Physics, Material Science, and related sciences. Essential preparation for research careers and technology company positions.

Transferable Skills: Analytical thinking and mathematical problem-solving highly valued in finance, consulting, and legal professions.

Chemistry A Level

Central science connecting biological and physical worlds. Tackles global challenges including energy demands, water scarcity, food production, and innovative medicine development. Essential for medical careers and highly complementary to sciences and humanities.

"80% of all nitrogen atoms in your body come from the Haber Process, a reaction that solved one of the most fundamental problems of the 20th Century — how to chemically fix nitrogen — this discovery changed the world: what will your discovery be?"

— James Copeland-Jordan, Head of Chemistry

Comprehensive Chemical Education

Lower Sixth Foundations: Introduction to organic, physical and inorganic chemistry. Core topics include atomic and electronic structure, chemical amounts, bonding and structure, equilibrium, energy, and fundamental organic chemistry concepts.

Upper Sixth Advanced Study: Advanced organic, physical and inorganic chemistry. Sophisticated topics including rates of reaction, redox chemistry, pH and buffers, free energy, complex synthesis, reaction mechanisms, and modern analytical techniques.

Central Laboratory Work: Weekly practical activities and regular demonstrations integrate theory with hands-on experience. Students develop essential practical skills and maintain laboratory notebooks.

Assessment & Practical Skills

Qualification: OCR Chemistry A Level (H432)
Examination Structure: Three examination papers assessing all course aspects
Synoptic Assessment: One paper assesses entire syllabus synoptically, including practical experiments
Practical Endorsement: Separate assessment of laboratory skills and competencies

Entry Requirements

  • Grade 7 in Chemistry or Grade 7/7 in Double Award Science
  • Strong mathematical foundation (Grade 6+ in Mathematics)
  • Interest in understanding chemical processes
  • Attention to detail for laboratory work

Enrichment Opportunities

  • Chemistry Olympiad competition
  • RSC Young Analyst competition
  • University of Bristol laboratory visits
  • Synthesis projects (benzocaine, caffeine isolation)

Essential for Medical & Scientific Careers

Medical Requirements: Chemistry A Level absolutely essential for Medicine, Dentistry, and Veterinary Science applications. Combined with Biology, provides scientific foundation required for medical school admission.

Scientific Pathways: Required for Agriculture, Biochemistry, Food Sciences, Oceanography, Pharmacy, and Physiology. Opens doors to cutting-edge research in materials science, environmental science, and pharmaceutical development.

Complementary Studies: Excellent when combined with Mathematics, Physics, and Biology for science specialisation, or with English, Classics, Art and Humanities for broader intellectual development.

Biology A Level

Understanding life from molecular to ecosystem levels. Combines detailed scientific knowledge with practical investigation skills, preparing students for medical careers and providing analytical abilities valuable across diverse fields from law to journalism.

"Biology opens up our understanding of life in all its fascinating complexity, from the molecular machinery inside our cells to the intricate relationships between organisms and their environment."

— Biology Department, Cheltenham College

Comprehensive Life Sciences Curriculum

Lower Sixth Foundations: Detailed understanding of cell structure, biological molecules, disease and immunology, biodiversity, evolution and classification. These fundamental concepts provide scaffold for advanced study.

Upper Sixth Advanced Biology: Advanced biochemistry including photosynthesis and cellular respiration. Human body coordination through nervous and hormonal systems, plus advanced genetics covering latest scientific developments including epigenetics and microbiology.

Integrated Practical Work: Strong practical focus throughout both years, developing new skills including microbiology and gel electrophoresis. Field ecology work and laboratory investigations with detailed record-keeping.

Assessment & Practical Endorsement

Qualification: OCR Biology A Level (H420)
Examination Structure: Three examination papers including synoptic assessment
Practical Endorsement: Pass/Fail assessment of core practical skills evidenced in laboratory books
Synoptic Element: Integration of all syllabus aspects including experimental work over two years

Entry Requirements

  • Grade 7 in Biology or Grade 7/7 in Double Award Science
  • Strong interest in living systems
  • Attention to detail for practical work
  • Analytical thinking abilities

Academic Enrichment

  • Royal Society of Biology events
  • Biology Olympiad competition
  • Nancy Rothwell Biological Drawing competitions
  • Student-edited 'Discovery' Biology magazine

Medical & Healthcare Pathways

Healthcare Careers: Excellent choice for Medicine, Veterinary Science, Dentistry, Biochemistry, Environmental Science, and Pharmacy. Detailed understanding of life processes provides essential foundation knowledge for medical training.

Broader Applications: Biology combines excellently with Geography, Psychology, History, or Languages. Analytical and application skills valuable in law, politics, business, and journalism, particularly in areas requiring scientific literacy.

Research Pathways: Preparation for biological research careers, environmental conservation, biotechnology, and pharmaceutical development. Practical skills and scientific method training highly valued by universities and employers.

Computer Science A Level

Developing analytical thinking and creative problem-solving through computational methods. Combines theoretical computer science with practical programming, preparing students for digital economy while fostering innovative thinking and systematic solution development.

"Computer Science is fundamentally about problem-solving through computational thinking - breaking down complex challenges and designing elegant, efficient solutions that can transform how we work and live."

— Computer Science Department, Cheltenham College

Comprehensive Computing Curriculum

Theoretical Foundations: Principles of computation and algorithms, computer programming fundamentals, machine data representation, computer systems architecture, computer organisation, and communications networking. Understanding underlying principles driving modern computing.

Practical Application: Extensive programming experience developing creative solutions to real-world problems. Database design and management, understanding consequences and ethics of computing technology in modern society.

Problem-Solving Focus: Computational thinking at core - modelling, analysing, and designing solutions through systematic approaches. Emphasis on precision, creativity, and logical reasoning essential for effective programming and system design.

Assessment Structure

Qualification: OCR Computer Science A Level
Structure: Two examined units plus one substantial coursework programming project
Coursework Project: Individual programming project completed in Upper Sixth demonstrating technical skills and creative problem-solving
Units: Computer Systems, Algorithms and Programming, plus Programming Project

Entry Requirements & Preparation

  • Strong logical reasoning and analytical thinking
  • Mathematics A Level highly recommended
  • Interest in problem-solving and technology
  • Attention to detail and systematic thinking

University Preparation

  • Essential for Computer Science degrees
  • Valuable for Engineering courses
  • Increasingly relevant for business and economics
  • Supports applications to competitive universities

Technology & Innovation Careers

Core Technology Roles: Direct pathway to Computer Science, Software Engineering, and Information Systems degrees. Essential preparation for careers in technology companies, software development, and digital innovation.

Interdisciplinary Applications: Computing expertise increasingly valuable across all sectors. Healthcare informatics, financial technology, environmental modelling, and business analytics all require computational thinking skills.

Future-Ready Skills: Artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, and data science transforming every industry. Computer Science provides foundation for understanding and contributing to these emerging fields.
