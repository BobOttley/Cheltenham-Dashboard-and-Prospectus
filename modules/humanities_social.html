English Literature A Level

Engaging with the centuries-long endeavor of great writers to capture the breadth and diversity of human experience. Literature explores love, morality, friendship, betrayal, ambition, justice, and fundamental questions of what it means to be human.

"Great literature captures all the joys and challenges of human experience. We read and instantly know ourselves and our world a little better."

— Sarah McCann, Head of English

Course Structure & Literary Exploration

Paper 1 Foundation: Study of Shakespeare (usually Hamlet or Coriolanus), poetry collections or extended forms (recent choices include Christina Rossetti, John Milton's Paradise Lost), and drama texts such as A Doll's House by Henrik Ibsen. Develops skills in close analysis, comparative judgment, and evaluation of biographical, historical, and social contexts.

Paper 2 Genre Study: Wide-ranging exploration of literary genres through two novels. Choose from American Literature (1880-1940), The Gothic, or Dystopian Literature. This comparative approach deepens understanding of literary traditions and their evolution.

Upper Sixth Coursework: Two independent essays on 20th and 21st century texts. Recent selections include The History Boys by Alan Bennett, The Sense of an Ending by Julian Barnes, A Streetcar Named Desire by Tennessee Williams, Rebecca by Daphne Du Maurier, and The Bell Jar by Sylvia Plath.

Assessment & Academic Rigor

Qualification: OCR English Literature A Level (H472)
Skills Development: Close textual analysis, comparative literary criticism, historical contextualization
Academic Enhancement: Literary Society (LitSoc) with Dr Davidson, Cheltenham Literature Festival attendance, theatre trips to Stratford-upon-Avon and London

University & Career Pathways

University Applications: Excellent preparation for English Literature, Creative Writing, Journalism, and Law degrees. Develops analytical writing and critical thinking skills highly valued by admissions tutors.

Career Applications: Journalism, Publishing, Media, Education, Law, Civil Service, and Creative Industries. The communication and analytical skills transfer across all professional sectors.

History A Level

Enthusiastic and energetic engagement with the past, developing skills of source analysis, interpretation synthesis, evidence evaluation, argument construction, and sophisticated written expression. Complex conceptual thinking that is both challenging and rewarding.

"History allows our students to access both the heady sense of escapism to be found in the 'rich tapestry' of the past, at the same time as it develops in them the means to participate intelligently in the world."

— Jo Doidge-Harrison, Head of History

Historical Periods & Analytical Skills

The Tudors: England 1485-1603: Exploring the scheming, venality, unholy Church activities, and military challenges including the Spanish Armada. Develops understanding of political, religious, and social transformation in early modern England.

The American Dream: Reality and Illusion 1945-1980: Seven presidents navigating economic boom and bust, civil rights explosion, student protest, and the military-industrial complex. Provides striking contrast and comparative analytical opportunities.

Independent Research Project: 20% coursework component developing historical research and referencing skills through a 4,500-word independent investigation. Recent topics range from 'Impact of Fashion by Coco Chanel and Dior' to 'Catalan Independence' and 'Life under the Stasi in the GDR.'

Assessment & Academic Standards

Qualification: AQA History A Level (7042)
Assessment Structure: 80% examination (two papers) + 20% coursework
Skills Focus: Source analysis, interpretation synthesis, evidence evaluation, sophisticated argumentation
Academic Preparation: Excellent foundation for university-level historical study and research

University & Career Applications

University Pathways: History, Politics, International Relations, Law, and Archaeology degrees. The research and analytical skills developed are highly valued by competitive universities.

Professional Applications: Law, Civil Service, Journalism, Museum and Heritage work, Education, Research, and Policy Analysis. Historical thinking skills transfer to many career paths.

Geography A Level

Exploiting natural curiosity about major contemporary issues in an interconnected world. Developing transferable skills including numeracy, literacy, investigation, research, problem-solving, logical reasoning, teamwork, and statistical analysis for real-world applications.

"Geographers are literate, numerate, empathetic, independent thinkers who are able to apply theories and models to real world places, people and environment."

— Fraser Dobney, Head of Geography

Comprehensive Geographical Study

Physical Systems: Interdependence between humans and land, oceans, atmosphere. Investigation of coastal environments, water/carbon cycles, anthropogenic climate change causes, and mitigation management strategies.

Human Interactions: People and place interactions across scales. Urban landscapes, inequality, rebranding, global migration, and human rights exemplifying sustainable management needs.

Geographical Debates: Synoptic understanding drawing from multiple topic areas to explain dynamic planetary events. Environmental and economic issues through Disease Dilemmas and Hazardous Earth topics.

Fieldwork & Independent Investigation

Qualification: OCR Geography A Level (H481)
Investigative Geography: 4,000-6,000 word independent investigation report
Fieldwork Foundation: Data collection during Summer Term residential field trip to Nettlecombe, Somerset
Regular Field Studies: Two field trips each Autumn and Spring Term plus four-day residential preparation

Applied Geography Careers

Environmental Careers: Environmental Science, Urban Planning, Conservation, Climate Change Analysis, and Sustainability Consulting. Geography provides excellent preparation for environmental and development careers.

Business Applications: Market Research, International Development, Logistics, and Spatial Analysis. The analytical and research skills are highly valued across business sectors.

Economics A Level

Far beyond numbers and stock markets - the study of our world from social, financial, environmental, and cultural perspectives. Always topical, Economics provides transferable analytical and evaluative skills applicable wherever you choose to study, work, or live.

"The study of A Level Economics helps young people better understand the world around them."

— Stephanie Burns, Head of Economics

Microeconomics & Macroeconomics

Microeconomics Foundation: Study of how individuals and companies make decisions to allocate scarce resources. Understanding price formation in markets ranging from oil to labor. Analysis of market structures, consumer behavior, and business decision-making processes.

Macroeconomics Scope: Study of whole economy behavior focusing on achieving stable economic growth with low unemployment, inequality, and uncertainty. Understanding fiscal and monetary policy, international trade, and economic development patterns.

Global Perspective: Whatever breaking news appears on Financial Times front pages, Economics understanding provides appreciation of significance. Essential skills for navigating our technological society's economic complexities.

Assessment & Academic Requirements

Qualification: OCR Economics A Level (H460)
Mathematical Requirement: Grade 7 in Mathematics essential
Assessment Structure: Three 2-hour examination papers combining micro and macro theory
Skills Emphasis: Quantitative analysis, balanced essay writing, evidence-based argumentation

Economics Career Applications

Financial Services: Banking, Investment Management, Economic Analysis, and Policy Development. Economics provides excellent preparation for finance and business careers.

Public Sector: Civil Service, Economic Policy, International Development, and Research. The analytical skills are highly valued in government and NGO sectors.

Government & Politics A Level

Current, accessible, and ever-changing study of governments, international relations, institutions, and decision-making processes in UK and USA. A fantastic way to learn what shapes our world and develop skills to participate as informed citizens in modern democracy.

"Politics is a vibrant and dynamic subject that helps students understand how power operates in the modern world and develops their ability to think critically about the institutions that govern our lives."

— Politics Department, Cheltenham College

Comprehensive Political Analysis

UK Politics & Government: Democracy, representation, participation; political parties and electoral systems; voting behavior and media; UK constitution; parliament; prime minister and executive; devolved bodies; relationships between executive, legislature, and judiciary.

US Government & Politics: US constitution and federalism; Congress; presidency; Supreme Court and civil rights; political parties and interest groups. Comparative analysis between US and UK systems developing sophisticated political understanding.

Political Philosophy: Deep exploration of political ideas including conservatism, liberalism, socialism, plus ecologism or feminism. Historical development and contemporary relevance of political thought.

Assessment & Analytical Skills

Qualification: Pearson Edexcel Politics A Level (9PL0)
Assessment Structure: Three separate papers - UK Politics, UK Government, US Government and Politics
Essay Requirements: Persuasive essays on core content plus evaluative essays on political ideas
Source Analysis: Extended source interpretation and comparative analysis skills

Political Career Pathways

Public Service: Civil Service, Local Government, Political Research, Policy Analysis, and International Relations. Politics provides excellent preparation for public sector careers.

Media & Communication: Political Journalism, Broadcasting, Public Relations, and Campaign Management. The analytical and communication skills are highly valued in media sectors.

Psychology A Level

Scientific study of mind and behavior - understanding yourself, people around you, social influences, and the differences between individuals. Fascinating exploration of human nature versus nurture debate and what makes us uniquely human.

"Psychology will not teach you to be a mind-reader, but it will help you understand your fellow humans a little better!"

— Tricia Norman, Head of Psychology

Scientific Study of Human Behavior

Core Topics: Attachment formation in childhood, memory models and forgetting factors, eye witness testimony accuracy, biological psychology examining brain structure and nervous system functions, brain localization, and biological rhythm investigation.

Research Methods: Practical psychologist training including research design, statistical analysis, and result interpretation. Introduction to different psychological approaches and perspectives, comparing various methodological approaches.

Contemporary Applications: Individual differences examining abnormality definitions, characteristics of phobias, depression, OCD, and treatment approaches. Understanding psychological principles applied to everyday life and clinical practice.

Scientific Assessment & Practical Skills

Qualification: AQA Psychology A Level (7182)
Entry Requirements: Grade 6 in Science (Biology preferred) and Mathematics
Assessment Structure: Three 2-hour examination papers focusing on different psychological domains
Practical Component: Essential statistical analysis and research methodology skills development

Psychology Career Applications

Healthcare Careers: Clinical Psychology, Counseling, Mental Health Services, and Healthcare Management. Psychology provides excellent foundation for helping professions.

Business Applications: Human Resources, Marketing, Consumer Behavior Analysis, and Organizational Psychology. Understanding human behavior is valuable across all business sectors.

Extended Project Qualification (EPQ)

Opportunity to step away from A Level syllabus confines and undertake extended, in-depth study of a topic of personal interest. Developing sophisticated research and project management skills that prepare students superbly for university-level study.

"The EPQ develops skills of reading, research, review, analysis, writing and evaluation essential for university study and careers."

— Cheltenham College EPQ Programme

Independent Research Excellence

Project Scope: Students work independently within small supervision groups to research and produce a dissertation or practical artefact in an area of personal interest beyond syllabus confines. Topics chosen entirely by students, allowing exploration of genuine intellectual passions.

Skill Development: Essential university preparation including logical thinking about arguments, counter-arguments, and rival theories; sensitivity in use of language; precision in point of view expression; reflective study approach; and critical evaluation.

Assessment Timeline: All students taking three A Levels are required to undertake EPQ, submitted at end of Spring Term in Lower Sixth. Those taking four A Levels may complete EPQ in their own time.

University-Level Research Skills

Qualification: AQA Extended Project Qualification (7993)
Assessment: Externally assessed project equivalent to half an A Level
Submission: End of Spring Term, Lower Sixth (for 3 A Level students)
University Value: UCAS points awarded, highly valued by admissions tutors for demonstrating independent learning

Lifelong Learning & Academic Excellence

University Preparation: EPQ mirrors university dissertation process, developing essential skills in research methodology, academic writing, and independent thinking. Students learn to manage long-term projects and evaluate sources critically.

Personal Development: Opportunity to explore genuine intellectual interests beyond curriculum constraints. Students develop confidence in independent learning and sophisticated research skills applicable across all fields.

Academic Recognition: Universities increasingly value EPQ as evidence of student initiative and academic potential. Project provides excellent discussion material for university interviews.
