Three Specialist Areas Rotation

Ten-Week Rotation System: Students experience creativity within the same overarching theme through rotations in three specialist areas, ensuring comprehensive exposure to different artistic mediums and techniques.

Fine Art: Exploring painting, drawing, and mixed media techniques, developing fundamental artistic skills and personal creative expression through traditional and contemporary approaches.

Ceramics: Hands-on experience with clay, learning forming, glazing, and firing techniques while exploring three-dimensional artistic expression and sculptural concepts.

Printmaking: Introduction to various printing techniques including relief, intaglio, and screen printing, developing understanding of multiple reproduction and artistic mark-making.

Creative Development & Assessment

Formative Assessment: Students are formatively assessed throughout each rotation with regular verbal feedback as work develops, building confidence and artistic understanding.

Online Portfolio: Maintaining an online portfolio where specialist teachers provide guidance and review progress, documenting artistic journey and development.

Research & Analysis: Training in researching artists and their work, analyzing and developing personal theories about artistic responses, connecting personal ideas to broader artistic contexts.

Philosophy & Approach

Risk-Taking Environment: Students are encouraged to take creative risks, becoming stronger, more confident and expressive individuals through exploration and experimentation.

Independent Learning: Nurturing independent learners who take possession of their work and ideas, recognizing that all outcomes, however seemingly insignificant, can lead to significant artistic growth.

Perfect for [Child's Name]

In our Design Technology programme, you'll develop practical skills while learning to solve real-world problems through innovative design.

Autumn Term - Vase Project Series

Textiles Vase Holder: Students create a mini vase holder utilizing sewing machines, eyelet machines, templates, tailors chalk, pressing, measuring, and top stitching techniques, learning fundamental textile skills.

Resistant Materials Vase: Rotation into timber work creating an alternative vase design in wood, learning dowel joints, butt joints, understanding jig importance, drilling, sanding, and finishing techniques.

Graphics Communication: Learning to draw and communicate designs using isometric and 2-point perspective drawing, essential skills for design visualization and communication.

Spring & Summer Terms - Storage Projects

Textiles - Laptop Case: Designing and developing a laptop case, applying textile skills learned in Autumn term to a practical, functional product with contemporary relevance.

Resistant Materials - Desk Organizer: Constructing a desk tidy or jewelry box, developing woodworking skills and understanding of storage solutions and functional design principles.

Graphics - 3D Modeling: Creating hand-cut card models, laser-cut designs, and 3D printed models, culminating in a small trinket box in card or MDF, introducing modern manufacturing techniques.

Design & Development
