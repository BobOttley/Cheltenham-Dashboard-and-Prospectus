English

Four periods per week developing reading, writing, speaking, and critical analysis skills. The course includes novel study, Shakespeare, and poetry, with drama appreciation opportunities integrated throughout.

Perfect for [Child's Name]'s Development

English will help develop the articulate communication and analytical writing skills that will serve throughout academic career and into university life.

"Drama is not taught in the Third Form, although the English curriculum gives the opportunity for developing an appreciation of the subject."

— Third Form Curriculum Guide

Your English Journey

[Child's Name]'s University Preparation

English will develop critical analysis and essay writing skills essential for university applications. Strong English grades demonstrate the communication abilities universities value across all disciplines.

Building Humanities Foundation

English provides the analytical writing and critical thinking foundation that complements History and Geography studies, developing skills that strengthen university applications.

Developing Leadership Communication

English develops articulate speaking and persuasive writing skills essential for leadership roles through presentation opportunities that build confidence.

Creative Expression

Creative writing elements, including participation in writing competitions, provide outlets for artistic expression whilst developing technical writing skills.

Course Structure

Autumn Term: Novel study with a special textbook written specifically for Cheltenham College pupils by Dr. Davidson, helping develop descriptive habits, grammar, and punctuation skills.

Spring Term: Shakespeare study where teachers share favorite passages from the Bard's work, building confidence with Shakespeare's language and legacy.

Summer Term: Poetry exploration, reading contemporary and traditional verse, including poems from the Hannam Clarke recitation prize.

Skills Development & Assessment

Analytical Writing: Learning to structure analytical essays and write creative, informative, and persuasive pieces in response to various stimuli.

Speaking & Listening: Individual presentations and group discussions developing articulate speaking and careful listening skills.

Reading & Analysis

  • Novel analysis and interpretation
  • Shakespeare language study
  • Poetry appreciation and analysis
  • Drama integration and appreciation

Creative & Critical Writing

  • Creative writing prize competition
  • Analytical essay structure
  • Grammar and punctuation skills
  • Reading Diploma participation

GCSE Preparation & Beyond

Foundation for GCSEs: Prepares students for both English Language and English Literature GCSEs through comprehensive skill development.

Drama Opportunities: While Drama is not separately taught, presentations on the subject are given prior to GCSE selection in February.

History

Two periods per week exploring historical periods, developing source analysis skills, and understanding causation, change, and continuity in human societies.

[Child's Name]'s Analytical Skills Journey

History will develop critical analysis and evidence evaluation skills, building the intellectual rigor needed for university aspirations.

"History develops essential skills in critical thinking, evidence evaluation, and understanding how the past shapes our present world."

— History Department

Your History Journey

Historical Analysis for University Success

History develops analytical and essay-writing skills highly valued by universities. Source evaluation and argument construction abilities transfer directly to university-level studies.

Humanities Excellence Foundation

As a core humanities subject, History provides essential skills in critical analysis, argument construction, and evidence evaluation that support success across all humanities subjects.

Leadership Through Historical Understanding

History develops understanding of leadership through different eras and cultures. Study of historical leaders and decision-making provides insights applicable to modern leadership challenges.

Historical Skills Development

Source Analysis: Students learn to evaluate historical sources critically, understanding bias, reliability, and usefulness while developing skills in historical investigation.

Chronological Understanding: Building knowledge of key historical periods and events, understanding cause and consequence, change and continuity.

Historical Writing: Developing skills in historical essay writing, argument construction, and synthesizing information from multiple sources.

Form Teaching Approach

Mixed-Ability Groups: History is taught in form groups, allowing students to benefit from diverse perspectives and ideas from a wide range of peers.

Key Historical Skills

  • Source evaluation and analysis
  • Cause and consequence assessment
  • Change and continuity understanding
  • Historical interpretation comparison

Assessment Methods

  • Source-based assignments
  • Extended essay writing
  • Research projects
  • Historical debate participation

Geography

Two periods per week exploring physical and human geography, developing fieldwork skills, and understanding environmental and social issues affecting our world.

Scientific & Analytical Approach

Geography combines scientific investigation with analytical skills, bridging science and humanities for well-rounded development.

"Geography connects students with the world around them, developing understanding of places, processes, and relationships between people and environments."

— Geography Department

Your Geography Journey

University Preparation

Geography develops the analytical and problem-solving skills valued by universities. The subject's combination of scientific and social science approaches provides excellent preparation for diverse university courses.

Bridging Sciences & Humanities

Geography uniquely combines interests by bridging scientific method with humanities analysis. Physical geography connects with sciences while human geography develops social science skills.

Global Leadership Perspective

Geography develops global awareness and understanding of complex world issues essential for modern leadership. Environmental and social challenges require the analytical skills Geography teaches.

Physical & Human Geography

Physical Geography: Students explore natural processes including weather and climate, landforms and landscapes, and natural hazards, developing understanding of Earth's physical systems.

Human Geography: Investigation of population dynamics, urbanization, economic activities, and development issues, understanding how human activities shape environments.

Geographical Skills: Map reading, data analysis, statistical techniques, and fieldwork methodologies essential for geographical investigation.

Form-Based Learning

Mixed-Ability Teaching: Geography benefits from having a variety of pupils in each form group, encouraging diverse perspectives on geographical issues.

Core Geographic Topics

  • Weather and climate patterns
  • Landform processes and landscapes
  • Population and settlement
  • Economic activities and development

Practical Skills

  • Map work and spatial analysis
  • Data collection and presentation
  • Fieldwork investigation techniques
  • Statistical analysis and interpretation

TPE (Religious Studies)

Two periods per week exploring religious beliefs, ethics, and philosophy, developing understanding of world religions and moral reasoning skills within form groups.

Ethical Reasoning & Cultural Understanding

TPE develops critical thinking about values and ethics, essential skills for leadership and university-level discourse.

"TPE develops students' understanding of diverse religious traditions and ethical frameworks, encouraging thoughtful reflection on fundamental questions of meaning and morality."

— Religious Studies Department

Your TPE Journey

University Preparation

TPE develops the ethical reasoning and cultural understanding valued by universities. The critical thinking and debate skills transfer across all disciplines.

Philosophy & Critical Thinking

TPE adds philosophical depth to humanities studies, developing reasoning skills and cultural awareness that enhance understanding across all humanities subjects.

Ethical Leadership

TPE develops the ethical reasoning and cultural sensitivity essential for modern leadership. Understanding diverse perspectives and moral frameworks prepares students for leading in a global context.

Religious Traditions & Ethics

World Religions: Students explore major world religions including Christianity, Islam, Judaism, Hinduism, Buddhism, and Sikhism, understanding beliefs, practices, and their impact on societies.

Moral Philosophy: Exploring ethical dilemmas and moral reasoning, understanding different approaches to right and wrong, and developing skills in ethical analysis.

Contemporary Issues: Applying religious and ethical perspectives to modern challenges including environmental ethics, medical ethics, and social justice issues.

Skills & Assessment

Form Group Teaching: TPE is taught in form groups alongside English, History, and Geography, allowing for integrated discussions and diverse perspectives.

Core Areas of Study

  • Major world religions exploration
  • Ethical theory and application
  • Philosophy of religion
  • Contemporary moral issues

Skills Development

  • Critical thinking and analysis
  • Reasoned argumentation
  • Respectful dialogue and debate
  • Cultural sensitivity and awareness
