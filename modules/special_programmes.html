Music

One period per week providing a highly varied and enjoyable introduction to musical topics. Everyone is a musician with something to contribute to the class 'ensemble'.

Music will provide creative expression and collaborative skills through performance and composition.

"Our ability to express ourselves has never been more important, and Music continues to allow this in a way that no other subject can."

— Shaun Pirttijarvi, Head of Academic Music

Music Opportunities for [Child's Name]

In our Music programme, you'll develop creative expression and collaborative skills through ensemble performance and digital composition.

Practical Music Making

Ensemble Performance: Individual keyboard work, class singing, percussion playing, and forming class ensembles using your own instruments. Music making is the main form of discourse for learning.

Performance Opportunities: Mini-recital slots included in lessons throughout the year, encouraging students to perform to each other with useful feedback and learning points.

Composition Projects: Individual and paired work exploring elements of music to create responses to various briefs using industry-standard software like Logic Pro and Sibelius.

Musical Understanding & GCSE Preparation

Elements of Music: Language of music developed to near-GCSE level throughout the year, enabling accurate analysis and mature understanding of how music works.

Cultural Context: Listening to and appraising live and recorded music from religious Renaissance music to current pop, English folk songs to Javanese gamelan.

Performance Skills

  • Individual keyboard work
  • Class singing and percussion
  • Ensemble music making
  • Mini-recital performances

Composition & Theory

  • Music technology with Logic Pro
  • Notation using Sibelius software
  • Creative composition projects
  • Music analysis and appreciation

Foundation Project Qualification (FPQ)

One period per week developing sophisticated research and writing skills. All Third Form pupils complete an independent project on a topic of personal interest.

FPQ will develop independent research skills essential for academic success and university preparation.

"An exciting and enriching addition to the curriculum which involves planning, researching and writing an independent project focused on a topic that is of personal interest to you."

— Third Form Curriculum Guide

FPQ Project for [Child's Name]

In the FPQ programme, you'll develop independent research skills through a personally meaningful project.

Independent Research Skills

Personal Interest Projects: Students choose a topic of personal interest and develop sophisticated research and writing skills with support from a dedicated FPQ mentor.

Academic Preparation: Develops skills essential for GCSE and A Level coursework, and prepares students for the Extended Project Qualification (EPQ) in Upper College.

External Certification: All pupils' FPQs are submitted for external certification by the AQA examination board, providing formal recognition of achievement.

Project Development Process

Mentored Support: Each student works with a member of staff who acts as their FPQ mentor, providing guidance throughout the project development process.

Research Skills

  • Topic selection and refinement
  • Academic research methods
  • Source evaluation and analysis
  • Evidence gathering techniques

Writing & Presentation

  • Academic writing structure
  • Argument development
  • Citation and referencing
  • Project presentation skills

Floreat (Wellbeing Programme)

One period per week focusing on holistic development - emotionally, socially, and personally. Explores 'Myself', 'Myself and Others', and 'Myself and the World'.

Floreat will support your personal development and wellbeing during this important stage of your life.

"The Floreat programme aims to help you flourish holistically by developing emotionally, socially, and growing in your understanding of yourselves, others and your world."

— Floreat Programme Guide

Personal Development for [Child's Name]

In Floreat, you'll develop emotional intelligence and personal resilience during this important stage of your development.

Myself - Personal Development

Physical Health: Learning about maintaining physical health through good sleep routines, healthy eating, and regular exercise, plus digital health awareness.

Growth Mindset: Developing belief that potential can be developed through dedication, reflection, and positive response to feedback, viewing challenges as opportunities.

Mental Health: Understanding and managing stress, building resilience, and developing emotional intelligence and self-awareness.

Myself and Others - Relationships

Respect and Responsibility: Key themes of respect for oneself and others, taking responsibility for actions, and developing tolerance, empathy, and kindness.

Social Skills: Assertion and bravery in social situations, understanding peer pressure and group influence on risk-taking behavior, particularly relevant to teenage brain development.

Relationships Education: Comprehensive relationships and sex education forming a key part of the programme, delivered age-appropriately.

Myself and the World - Global Citizenship

Environmental Awareness: Topics including recycling, sustainability, and environmental responsibility, connecting personal actions to global impact.

Rights and Law: Understanding rights, responsibilities, and legal frameworks that govern society and personal behavior.

Exam Preparation: Practical sessions on exam preparation techniques and managing exam stress, particularly relevant before year-end assessments.

Effort brings reward
