Academic Structure & Learning

In each week there are 39 periods across 17 different subjects, providing breadth and depth to establish strong foundations for IGCSE and GCSE studies.

Tailored for Your Interests

With your interests in mind, you'll find Third Form's broad curriculum particularly engaging as it provides the foundation for future specialisation.

Sets and Forms Organisation

Sets: Mathematics, Sciences (Biology, Chemistry, Physics), and Modern Languages are taught in ability sets to provide targeted support at appropriate learning pace. Movement between sets occurs regularly based on progress assessments.

Your Academic Journey

Sciences Excellence Path

[Child's Name] will thrive in our dedicated Science sets across Biology, Chemistry, and Physics. With three separate teachers specializing in each subject, developing the deep understanding needed for A-Level success and university applications in STEM fields.

  • Biology Set: From cell structure to ecosystems, building toward A-Level Biology
  • Chemistry Set: Atomic theory to organic chemistry foundations
  • Physics Set: Forces and energy leading to advanced physics concepts
Mathematics Acceleration

With strong mathematical aptitude, placement in Set 1 Mathematics means working toward taking IGCSE Mathematics at the end of Fourth Form, typically achieving Grade 9 before progressing to Additional Mathematics in Fifth Form.

Third Form: Advanced algebra, geometry, and problem-solving

Fourth Form: IGCSE Mathematics examination (Grade 9 target)

Fifth Form: Additional Mathematics preparation for A-Level

Leadership Development Through Academic Structure

Interest in leadership will be naturally developed through our setting system. High-achieving students in top sets often become peer mentors, helping classmates and taking on presentation responsibilities that build confidence and communication skills.

Russell Group Preparation

With Russell Group aspirations, the setting system ensures receiving the rigorous academic challenge needed for competitive university applications. Top sets maintain the pace and depth required for A*-A grades at A-Level.

Forms: [Child's Name] will be placed in one of our seven forms (3M1, 3M2, 3P1, 3P2, 3P3, 3R1, 3R2) where they'll study English, Geography, History and TPE with the same group. This creates a supportive learning community where lasting friendships develop while exploring humanities subjects that complement scientific interests.

Your Form Experience

In their form group, pupils will engage in collaborative projects, discussions, and presentations that develop the communication skills valued by Russell Group universities. The consistent group dynamic allows for taking on informal leadership roles and building confidence in expressing ideas.

Mixed Arrangements: Art, Music, Design Technology, Computing, FPQ, Skills Programme, and Floreat are taught in mixed-ability groups, providing opportunities to work with students across all academic levels and develop the collaborative skills essential for university and career success.

Setting Policy & Support

Sets are determined initially by Common Entrance or College Scholarship performance but are reviewed regularly. Any set changes are made to better support your learning at an appropriate pace - never as punishment.

Mathematics Excellence

Pupils in Set 1 Maths aim to sit IGCSE at the end of Fourth Form, typically achieving Grade 9, before progressing to Additional Mathematics in Fifth Form - perfect for those with mathematical aptitude.

Professional Judgement

Teachers and Heads of Department carefully consider all set moves to ensure optimal learning support for each individual student.

Regular Review

Sets are reviewed throughout the year following assessments, with tutors notifying parents of any changes.

Languages Excellence

Our comprehensive two-language programme develops fluency across multiple languages, essential for global citizenship and competitive university applications. With German, French, and Spanish all available, you'll gain cultural awareness and communication skills valued by employers and universities worldwide.

Language Options Detail:

  • French: Available as both continuation (for those who studied it to CE) and potentially as beginners' option
  • Spanish: Available as both continuation (for those who studied it to CE) and as beginners' option
  • German: Offered as beginners' language with structured topic-based learning and clear grammatical progression
  • EAL: For overseas pupils whose native language is not English, studying EAL instead of continuation language

Immersion Opportunities: The department organizes educational trips to Montpellier (France), Salamanca (Spain), and Berlin exchanges (Germany), providing authentic immersion experiences.

Classical Studies: In addition to modern languages, all pupils study either Latin or Classical Civilisation (3 periods/week), connecting with ancient cultures and developing linguistic precision. Those studying Latin also receive a taster in Classical Greek during the Spring Term.

Pastoral Support & Tutors

Every pupil is allocated a Tutor who remains with them until Fifth Form, providing vital pastoral and academic support throughout Lower College.

Caring Environment

Our comprehensive pastoral care system ensures you'll receive the personal support needed to thrive academically and personally.

The Tutor System

Your Tutor has vital pastoral and academic roles, including teaching Floreat, meeting tutees briefly every morning, and having fortnightly one-to-one meetings to discuss progress and wellbeing.

First Port of Call: Your Tutor should be your first contact for all academic matters, providing continuity and support throughout your Lower College journey.

Floreat Wellbeing Programme

The Floreat programme addresses personal development, mental health awareness, relationships, and life skills. Delivered through tutor-led sessions, it prepares students for the challenges and opportunities of teenage years.

Personal Development

Building resilience, self-awareness, and emotional intelligence through structured programmes.

Wellbeing Focus

Mental health awareness, stress management, and developing healthy relationships with peers and adults.

Morning Registration: Tutor period and Chapel provide daily touchpoints and community connection.

House System: House Registration, evening study time, and residential support create a supportive community environment.

Learning Support & Special Programmes

Comprehensive support systems ensure every pupil can achieve their potential, with specialist programmes for additional learning needs and academic enrichment.

Academic Excellence Support

Our support programmes ensure you achieve your full academic potential while developing essential research and study skills.

Support for Learning

Miss Suzanne Marquis, Head of Learning Support, oversees support arrangements for pupils with recognised learning needs. She coordinates testing of all new entrants and liaises with parents, previous schools, and College staff.

Foundation Project Qualification (FPQ)

All Third Form pupils study the FPQ - an exciting and enriching addition developing sophisticated research and writing skills essential for GCSE, A Level, and the Extended Project Qualification in Upper College.

Independent Research

Pupils plan, research, and write an independent project on a topic of personal interest, supported by a dedicated FPQ mentor.

External Certification

All FPQs are submitted for external certification by the AQA examination board, providing formal recognition of achievement.

Skills Programme & Computing

Skills Programme: Dedicated period developing study skills, time management, and organisational capabilities essential for academic success.

Computing: Introduction to College ICT systems, digital literacy modules, and coding. The scheme is differentiated to develop existing skills and accommodate varying levels of prior experience.

Fourth & Fifth Form Preparation

Third Form provides the foundation for informed GCSE choices and successful transition to examination years, with ongoing support and guidance.

University Preparation

Third Form begins your journey toward university success, with early career reflection and strategic planning for your academic future.

GCSE Subject Selection

February Decision Point: You will choose your GCSE/IGCSE subjects in February of Third Form, starting those courses at the beginning of Fourth Form with full details provided at Christmas.

Informed Choices: Third Form's broad curriculum ensures you experience sufficient depth in subjects to make informed decisions about specialisation.

Business & Economics Pathway

While not available in Third Form, Business Studies and Economics are popular GCSE choices, with presentations given to help you make informed decisions about these subjects.

Subject Presentations: Sports Science and Drama, though not taught in Third Form, are popular GCSE choices with presentations given prior to selection.

Transition to Examination Years

Increased Independence: Prep increases and pupils learn to plan ahead, finding optimal study times as House study time alone becomes insufficient.

Career Reflection: Weekly tutorial programme encourages thinking about career aspirations and university entrance strategy, including work experience, reading, and trips.

Sixth Form Entry

Expectation of Grade 6 or equivalent in at least five GCSE/IGCSE subjects - a reasonable hurdle that the majority far exceed.

Work Ethic Development

Developing habits of hard work from Third Form start is vital for long-term academic success.
