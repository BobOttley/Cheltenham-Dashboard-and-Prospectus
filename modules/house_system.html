Ashmead

Girls' Boarding • Est. 2000

Our apple emblem reflects our site's history as the former Boyne House orchard. Opened by HRH The Princess Royal, Ashmead creates a family environment where food and community are central.

Housemistress: Ester Leach

Ester lives in Ashmead with her husband Matthew (a chef) and twin girls Zara and Tula. The family's passion for food makes culinary activities a highlight of House life.

65 Students
2000 Founded
5 Apple Families

🏛️ Royal Opening

HRH The Princess Royal officially opened this modern, purpose-built girls' boarding house in October 2000, creating a landmark moment in College history.

🍳 Culinary Culture

Food is a key aspect of Ashmead life. With our Housemistress's husband being a chef, any House activity involving food is always a tremendous success.

🍎 Apple Heritage

Named after the famous Gloucestershire apple Ashmead's Kernel, first planted in the early 18th century. There are still two Ashmead's Kernel trees in the House grounds today.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 House Families

Girls are organized into House families, each named after different types of apples, fostering vertical mentoring relationships across all year groups.

Weekend Activities

Girls' favourite weekend activities are s'mores around the fire pit, cooking, baking, and family competitions. Sunday trips to Cheltenham town centre are also popular.

Boyne House

Boys' Boarding • Est. 1840

The oldest boarding house at College, Boyne House has a distinguished history spanning over 180 years. Known as "Brooksmithites," our boys develop strong values of manners, effort, and community spirit.

Housemaster: Andrew Straiton

Andrew lives in the House with his wife Megan (a GP) and three children (Freddie, Beatrice, Annabelle). Together with their tutor team, they instill values of manners and effort.

🏛️ Heritage & Traditions

The oldest boarding house at College, with distinguished alumni in politics, exploration, scientific research, and business. The owl emblem comes from the 1973 artistic performances award tie.

🎯 Special Activities

  • Three Peaks Challenge expeditions
  • Devizes to Westminster Canoe Race
  • Hill walking and outdoor adventures
  • BoyneFest charity event (silent disco)
  • Afghan Refugee Cricket Project hosting
House Charity: James Hopkins Trust

Supporting families in need of respite care through BoyneFest fundraising events, DJ sets, and community engagement activities.

Chandos

Girls' Boarding • Est. 1981

The oldest girls' boarding house with the distinctive ladybird emblem. We were the first to introduce House ties, now adopted by all Houses.

Housemaster: Alex Henocq

Alex is a passionate Chemistry teacher with wife Mary and son Luca, creating a boarding house family that values everyone as individuals.

🏆 Pioneering Traditions

Chandos introduced the first House tie, recognizing pupils who show significant commitment to the House. This tradition has been adopted by all other Houses.

📈 Growth & Excellence

From eight Sixth Form girls in 1981 to 80 girls today spanning Third Form to Upper Sixth, developing personal skills and talents.

Christowe

Boys' Boarding • Est. 1866

"Boyceites" embrace the house motto: "Nil Desperandum!" (Never Despair!) with its distinctive skull and crossbones emblem from the Royal Lancers.

Housemaster: Dan Paynter

Dan is a Physics teacher who lives with wife Harriet (Religious Studies teacher) and son Rory, developing boys who throw themselves wholeheartedly into opportunities.

🎯 House Motto

"Nil Desperandum!" (Never Despair!) - embodying the Christowe spirit of resilience, determination, and wholehearted participation.

Queen's House

Girls' Day House

Our girls' day house promotes independence, respect, and belonging. We pride ourselves on inclusion and friendship across all year groups, creating lasting connections.

Housemistress: Wandrille Vere

Supported by husband Will (Geography teacher) and daughters Eleanor and Eloïse. They believe in the all-round ethos ensuring each girl finds her niche.

👑 Royal Heritage

Queen Elizabeth II gave permission for the House to be renamed 'Queen's House' in 1991 to commemorate her visit. Officially opened by Anne Cadbury in June 1994.

🏛️ Georgian Architecture

Elegant Georgian building built in 1847 with wonderful views across College Lawn. Originally Linden House, purchased as part of College's 150th Anniversary.
